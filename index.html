About

I am a Visiting Research Student at the Tsung-Dao Lee Institute (Shanghai Jiao Tong University), working on ultra-high-energy gamma-ray astrophysics with LHAASO alongside Dr. Gwenael Giacinti and Dr. Hao Zhou.

I completed an integrated MPhys in Theoretical Physics at the University of St Andrews (2025) under Dr. Martin Dominik, and was a Gruber-funded Research Student at the European Southern Observatory working with Dr. Pooneh Nazari. I have also carried out RAS-funded research supervised by Dr. Indranil Banik.

My broader interests include γ-ray astronomy, protostellar systems, cosmology, and gravitational microlensing.

Research

Interests

My research interests span a wide array of subdisciplines in astronomy. First, I am currently studying protostellar systems, and how structure and evolution can bias chemical abundance measurments. In addition, I am interested in the Hubble Tension and the viability of various proposed solutions to alleviate it. Furthermore, I am actively working on expalining the observed ultra-high diffuse γ-ray excess that has been recently observed. Last but not least, I am finilising my master's thesis on using gravitational microlensing data alongside numerical and machine learning techniques to probe stellar atmospheres.

Chemistry of Protostellar Systems

My research on the chemistry of protostellar systems has been conducted at the European Southern Observatory alongside Dr. Pooneh Nazari, and aimed to understand recent mismatches between gas and ice chemical abundance ratios. I used radiative transfer modelling to test whether structure alone can explain these recent observations or whether complex chemistry is needed, and found that structural effects may introduce order of magnitude differences in the inferred collumn density ratios.
